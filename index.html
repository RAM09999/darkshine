Andhra Pradesh

Tirupati Balaji Temple

Location
Tirumala, near Tirupati, Chittoor district
Built / Era
Ancient shrine expanded by Pallava, Chola, Vijayanagara, and later rulers
Type
Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara
Best Time
September to February

Sri Venkateswara Temple stands on the Tirumala hills, part of the Seshachalam range. The shrine is one of India's most important pilgrimage centers, known for its disciplined darshan system, laddoo prasadam, golden vimana, and centuries of royal patronage. Its location, rituals, music, and devotional crowds make it a living example of South Indian temple culture.

Araku Valley

Location
Eastern Ghats, Alluri Sitharama Raju district
Built / Era
Natural valley; tourism developed around coffee, rail, and tribal culture
Type
Hill valley and cultural destination
Best Time
October to March

Araku Valley sits among forested hills and coffee plantations about 900 meters above sea level. Visitors come for viewpoints, Borra Caves nearby, waterfalls, tribal museums, and the train route that crosses tunnels and bridges. It is a refreshing nature escape from Visakhapatnam and a good place to learn about local Adivasi traditions.

Arunachal Pradesh

Tawang Monastery

Location
Tawang, western Arunachal Pradesh
Built / Era
Founded in 1680-1681 by Merak Lama Lodre Gyatso
Type
Tibetan Buddhist monastery
Best Time
March to June and September to October

Tawang Monastery is one of the largest Buddhist monasteries in India. It contains prayer halls, murals, thangkas, residential quarters, and a large gilded Buddha image. Perched above the Tawang valley, it is spiritually important to the Gelug school and visually unforgettable because of its Himalayan setting.

Assam

Kaziranga National Park

Location
Golaghat, Nagaon, and Karbi Anglong districts
Established
Declared reserved forest in 1908; national park status in 1974
Type
UNESCO wildlife national park
Best Time
November to April

Kaziranga protects floodplain grasslands, wetlands, and forests along the Brahmaputra. It is best known for the Indian one-horned rhinoceros, but also shelters elephants, wild water buffalo, swamp deer, tigers, and migratory birds. Safari routes reveal how annual floods shape the ecology of Assam.

Location

Built / Era
Present structure largely rebuilt in the 16th century by the Koch dynasty
Type
Shakti Peetha temple
Best Time
October to March; Ambubachi Mela in June

Kamakhya Temple is one of the most important Shakti shrines in India. Its distinctive beehive-like dome, tantric traditions, and hilltop location make it a powerful cultural site. Pilgrims visit the inner sanctum, while travelers also enjoy views over Guwahati and the Brahmaputra.

Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

Geographical Location
Agra, on the right bank of the Yamuna River
Construction Year
Construction began in 1632 and the main mausoleum was completed in 1648; the complex finished around 1653
Built By
Mughal emperor Shah Jahan for Mumtaz Mahal
Type
UNESCO marble mausoleum and garden complex

The Taj Mahal is one of the world's most admired monuments. Built from white Makrana marble, it combines Persian, Timurid, and Indian design traditions in a perfectly balanced composition. The central dome, four minarets, pietra dura inlay, Quranic calligraphy, mosque, jawab building, reflecting pools, and charbagh garden all work together to create its famous sense of harmony.

It is both a royal tomb and a symbol of love. The changing light at sunrise, sunset, and moonlit nights alters the color of the marble, which is why visitors often experience it as a living monument rather than a fixed structure.
